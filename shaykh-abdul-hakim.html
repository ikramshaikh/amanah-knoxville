Shaykh ‘Abd al-Ḥakīm Dickenson

Scholar of Islamic Sharī‘ah | Educator | Community Leader

Traditional Licensing and Scholarly Formation

Traditional Licensing and Mastery (Ijāzah & Sanad)

In July 2009, Shaykh ‘Abd al-Ḥakīm Dickenson achieved a formal Ijāzah and Sanad (traditional license and continuous chain of scholarly transmission) in Prophetic Traditions (Ḥadīth) and Islamic Sciences from the Institute of Islamic Education (IIE) in Elgin, Illinois. This certification represents the culmination of advanced textual mastery and authorizes the transmission of classical sacred knowledge.

Advanced Graduate Seminary Enrollment (2006 – 2009)

He completed intensive, full-time residency and advanced scholarly training at the Institute of Islamic Education (IIE) in Elgin, Illinois, focusing on both foundational and advanced text-based Islamic disciplines.

Foundational Seminarian Residency (1999 – 2003)

He pursued foundational, full-time Islamic studies across premier North American institutions, including:

  • Darul Uloom New York, Queens, New York (2002 – 2003)
  • Jamiyatul Uloom al Islamiyyah al Inamiyyah, Ajax, Ontario, Canada (1999 – 2001)

Senior Academic and Juristic Profile

Shaykh ‘Abd al-Ḥakīm Dickenson is an accomplished scholar of the Islamic Sharī‘ah, specializing in:

  • Classical Hanafi jurisprudence (Fiqh)
  • Legal theory (Uṣūl al-Fiqh)
  • Qur’anic exegesis (Tafsīr)

With over two decades of formal teaching and pastoral experience within the North American Deobandi educational framework, he has served as a senior professor in intensive ‘Ālim programs at leading institutions, including the Institute of Islamic Education (IIE) and Darul Qasim.

His academic work is supported by a formal Ijāzah and Sanad in Ḥadīth and Islamic Sciences, representing a continuous chain of transmission.

Community Leadership and Pastoral Counseling

Beyond his academic appointments, Shaykh ‘Abd al-Ḥakīm has extensive field experience serving as an Imām and Khaṭīb across multiple states, including Illinois, Kentucky, New York, and New Jersey.

His pastoral work emphasizes:

  • Family and youth guidance
  • Islamic conflict resolution
  • Marital counseling
  • Contemporary parenting

He bridges classical text-based mastery with practical, actionable guidance, helping Muslims navigate modern Western environments.
